Star Wars History

It is reasonable to say that the Star Wars franchise has had a massive effect on not only audiences, but on cinema and science fiction films as a whole. It is easily recognizable and people who have never seen the films can most likely pull out pieces of the films and be relatively accurate. And with new films and media being released, the franchise is still having an effect on new generations. Star Wars also has been around for nearly forty years (half of which passed between the releases of A New Hope and The Phantom Menace), which is plenty of time to build itself as the icon it deserves to be.

Important Dates

  • May 25, 1977: Star Wars (renamed in 1981 to Episode IV: A New Hope) is first released in theaters
  • May 21, 1980: Episode V: The Empire Strikes Back is released
  • May 25, 1983: Episode VI: The Return of the Jedi is released
  • 1997: A special edition of the Original Trilogy is released for its 20th Anniversay. This is where the conflict surrounding "Han shot first" arose as the new editions expanded the shot to show Greedo shooting first.
  • May 19, 1999: Episode I: The Phantom Menace, the first of the Prequel Trilogy, is released
  • May 16, 2002: Episode II: The Attack of the Clones is released
  • May 19, 2005: Episode III: The Revenge of the Sith, which highlights the transformation of Anakin Skywalker to Darth Vader, is released
  • August 15, 2008: The animated film Star Wars: The Clone Wars is released. The movie was followed by the Cartoon Network series of the same name, which ran for five and a half seasons.
  • October, 2012: Disney purchases Lucasfilm Ltd. as well as annouces the first film of the Sequel Trilogy
  • April, 2014: Disney and Lucasfilm Ltd. announce the decanonization of the Expanded Universe, creating the Legends series and opening the doors for the Sequel Trilogy and Anthology films
  • February 5, 2013: Disney announces the Anthology series, which are within the Star Wars Universe but do not cross with the current saga films
  • December 15, 2015: The first film in the Sequel Trilogy, Episode VII: The Force Awakens, is released in theaters
  • December 16, 2016: The release date for the first Anthology film, Rogue One: A Star Wars Story

On Special and Sound Effects

Audio Recording here

Star Wars was highly influential when it came to the development of special and sound effects. For example, the famous opening crawl was filmed by pulling an angled camera down the sheet containing all of the text for the crawl. George Lucas hired special effects expert John Stears to create the mechanical and electrical effects and Stears is responsible for the designs of R2-D2, C-3PO, and the famous aerial dogfight featured at the end of the film. Lucas also hired sound designer Ben Burtt to create the sound effects. Burtt's sound design was innovative, using common sounds to create the famous ones known from the film. For example, the lightsaber hum is a combination of an idling projector and television feedback while the iconic "pew pew" noise the blasters are famous for is a guy-wire being hit with a hammer. Burtt is also credited with creating the infamous "Wilhelm scream" heard when a stormtrooper falls on the Death Star and he won an Academy award for Special Acheivement as there was no award for sound design at the time. The score for all seven saga films has been composed by John Williams, who will also be composing the score for Episode VIII.
